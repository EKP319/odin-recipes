Cranberry Chicken

Description

Cranberry chicken is a quick and easy meal that everyone loves in our family! The chicken is baked to perfection in a savory sweet cranberry sauce with sweet bursts of dried cranberries and toasted pecan pieces. It is a simple meal that makes a statement, so it is sure to impress your guests! Cranberry chicken looks so elegant and beautiful like a Christmas dinner! It has all the flavor, and easy ingredients, and is baked in under an hour! The sweetness of cranberries and the savory flavor of chicken are the perfect combination you need for a comforting and fulfilling family dinner. It is the perfect option for an elegant or festive meal or a quick and simple meal! This chicken dish is sure to please!

Ingredients

  1. Chicken – Start with four large boneless chicken breasts, cut in half. You should have enough chicken to fill a 9×13 baking dish.
  2. Cranberry sauce – I like to use a can of whole-berry cranberry sauce, but you can use leftover homemade sauce from Thanksgiving or Christmas dinner.
  3. Dry onion soup mix – Use one packet of the dry onion soup mix. It has seasoning in it and flavor.
  4. Pecans – Chop pecans for a delicious crunch and texture.
  5. Craisins – These are soft dried cranberry that adds a little more texture and flavor to the cranberry mixture.
  6. Catalina salad dressing – It may sound crazy but you will use a whole bottle of Catalina salad dressing. We use a smaller bottle but for extra richness in flavor dump a larger bottle onto the chicken.

How to Prepare Cranberry Chicken

  1. Prepare chicken – Arrange the chicken in a baking pan, prepped with some cooking spray. If you’re using thighs with skin, make sure to put the skin side down.
  2. Layer – Spread cranberry sauce all over the chicken in a nice, thick layer over each piece of chicken. Sprinkle the onion soup packet, chopped pecans, and dried cranberries over the chicken. Then pour the Catalina salad dressing over the top.
  3. Cover and bake – Lightly cover the pan with aluminum foil and bake in a 350-degree oven for 45 minutes.
  4. Uncover and bake – Uncover the chicken and bake again for 15 more minutes so the sauce thickens and becomes a shiny glaze.
  5. Serve – Serve the extra sauce in a bowl and spoon over the chicken when serving. You can also garnish with a sprig of fresh rosemary.
